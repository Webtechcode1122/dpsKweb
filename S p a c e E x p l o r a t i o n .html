Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope-
The Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope, named after the renowned astrophysicist, is poised to revolutionize space exploration with a specific focus on dark matter. In collaboration with scientists, this telescope aims to unveil the secrets of dark matter, promising breakthroughs that will reshape our understanding of the universe. With cutting-edge technology and a dedicated team, it stands as a beacon in the quest to illuminate the elusive substance that dominates the cosmic landscape.The Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope (NGRST) is designed to revolutionize our understanding of the universe with its advanced capabilities, focusing on dark energy, dark matter, and exoplanet exploration. As a successor to the Hubble Space Telescope, the NGRST promises unparalleled insights into cosmic mysteries, providing a crucial lens for humanity's ongoing cosmic exploration.

Objectives of the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope:
1.Dark Energy and Dark Matter Exploration: Investigate the nature of dark energy and dark matter, aiming to unveil their properties and understand their role in shaping the cosmos.


2.Exoplanet Surveys: Conduct comprehensive surveys to discover and characterize exoplanets, including those in the habitable zone, contributing to our understanding of planetary systems beyond our own.


3.Cosmic Evolution: Study the formation and evolution of galaxies over cosmic time, providing insights into the processes that have shaped the large-scale structure of the universe.


4.Infrared Observations: Utilize its advanced infrared capabilities to peer through cosmic dust clouds, enabling the observation of celestial objects and phenomena that are otherwise obscured in visible light.
