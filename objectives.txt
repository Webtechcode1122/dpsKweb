Objectives of the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope: 

Dark Energy and Dark Matter Exploration: Investigate the nature of dark energy and dark matter, aiming to unveil their properties and understand their role in shaping the cosmos.

Exoplanet Surveys: Conduct comprehensive surveys to discover and characterize exoplanets, including those in the habitable zone, contributing to our understanding of planetary systems beyond our own.

Cosmic Evolution: Study the formation and evolution of galaxies over cosmic time, providing insights into the processes that have shaped the large-scale structure of the universe.

Infrared Observations: Utilize its advanced infrared capabilities to peer through cosmic dust clouds, enabling the observation of celestial objects and phenomena that are otherwise obscured in visible light.

General Astrophysics: Contribute to various areas of astrophysics, including the study of supernovae, the measurement of the Hubble constant, and the investigation of gravitational lensing.
